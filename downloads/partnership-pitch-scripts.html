The Partnership Pitch Scripts

Word-for-word scripts to land local partnerships that grow your text club, build your list, and create predictable revenue — without spending a dollar on ads.

Why this matters: The operators growing fastest aren't outspending their competitors. They're out-partnering them. One partnership can produce hundreds of warm contacts per month — people who already trust you before they ever visit your wash.

These scripts have been tested across hundreds of car wash locations in real conversations with real business owners. They work because they follow one principle: make the partner the hero.

Script 1: The Service Business Pitch

Best for: Oil change shops, mechanics, tire stores, auto detailers, body shops

Why it works: Their customers are already thinking about car care. A free wash feels like a natural extension of the service they just received. You're not interrupting — you're adding value to an experience they already trust.

The Walk-In Script

What to Say

"Hey, I'm [Your Name] from [Your Car Wash]. I own the wash over on [Street/Location]. I've got an idea I think could make your customers really happy — and it won't cost you a thing or take any extra time."

"Here's what I'd like to do. I want to give you a stack of free wash cards that you can hand out to your customers after their service. They get a free wash from us. You get the credit for surprising them with something nice. We handle everything."

"All your customer has to do is scan the QR code on the card. They get their free wash, and you look like the hero for giving it to them. I'll print the cards, bring them by, and replace them whenever you run low."

"Would you be open to trying it? I can have the cards here by [day]."

Pro Tip

Bring a sample card with you on the first visit. When people can see and hold the physical card, the conversation moves from abstract to concrete in seconds. It also shows you've already done the work — all they have to do is say yes.

If They Ask "What's In It for You?"

What to Say

"Great question. When your customer scans the card, they join our text club so we can send them their free wash code. That gives us a chance to stay in touch and invite them back. So you're giving your customer a great experience, and we get the chance to earn a new regular. Everybody wins."

The Sweetener (Optional)

What to Say

"I'd also love to offer your team free washes. Your employees can wash anytime — on us. Think of it as a small thank you for being a great partner."

Script 2: The Restaurant & Retail Pitch

Best for: Restaurants, coffee shops, gyms, hair salons, barbershops, boutiques, small retail chains

Why it works: These businesses see your ideal customers every week. Trust transfers instantly. When Chick-fil-A hands out your free wash card, it doesn't feel like marketing. It feels like a gift from a place they already love.

The Walk-In Script

What to Say

"Hey, I'm [Your Name] from [Your Car Wash]. I've been thinking about how we can do something cool together that makes your customers feel really appreciated."

"Here's what I have in mind. I want to give you free car wash cards that your team can hand out to customers. It could be a surprise with their order, a loyalty reward, a rainy-day pick-me-up — whatever feels right for your brand. Your customers get a free wash. You get the credit. We take care of everything."

"I'll design the cards, print them, and deliver them. Your team doesn't have to track anything or do any extra work. They just hand them out when the moment feels right."

"Would you be interested in trying it for a month and seeing how your customers respond?"

Making the Manager the Hero

What to Say

"What I've seen work really well is when managers use these as surprise-and-delight moments. A customer's been coming in every week? Hand them a free wash card. Someone's having a rough day? That card turns their afternoon around. It makes your team look incredible and costs you nothing."

Pro Tip

Offer to cross-promote. Tell the partner: "From time to time, I'd love to send a shout-out about your business to our text club. We have [X] people on our list and I think a lot of them already love your [restaurant/shop/gym]." This transforms the partnership from a one-way favor into a genuine two-way relationship.

Employee & Vehicle Sweetener

What to Say

"I'd also like to offer free washes for your employees and any company vehicles. If you've got catering vans, delivery cars, or company trucks — they can wash for free anytime. It's just a small way to say thanks for being a partner."

Script 3: The Nonprofit & School Fundraiser Pitch

Best for: Schools (PTA/PTO), sports teams, churches, youth groups, local nonprofits

Why it works: This path scales faster than any other because it taps into the largest networks in your community. Parents share fundraiser links with other parents. Teams share with supporters. Churches share with congregations. Every share brings warm contacts who are already in a positive emotional state when they buy.

The Email/Call Script

What to Say

"Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] from [Your Car Wash]. I work with local schools and organizations to help them raise money in a way that's easy, digital, and actually works."

"Here's how it works. We set up a custom fundraising page for [School/Organization Name]. Your supporters buy car wash packages at full price through the link. [School/Organization Name] keeps 50% of every sale. And we handle all the setup, tracking, and delivery."

"There's nothing for your team to manage. No physical product to store or distribute. No cash to collect. Supporters just click the link, buy the washes, and the money goes directly to your organization."

"Schools we've partnered with have raised [thousands of dollars] in just a few weeks. Would you be open to a quick conversation about how this could work for [School/Organization Name]?"

The Follow-Up Script (If They're Interested)

What to Say

"Great. Here's what happens next. I'll build your custom fundraising page. All you need to do is share the link with your community — email, text, social media, however you normally communicate. Every time someone buys a wash package, your organization earns 50%. I'll send you weekly updates so you can see exactly how the fundraiser is performing."

"The best part? This isn't a one-time thing. We can run it as often as you'd like — seasonally, for specific events, or as an ongoing fundraiser. It keeps working as long as you keep sharing the link."

Pro Tip

Start with one school. Run the fundraiser for 3–4 weeks. Document the results. Then use that success story to pitch every other school, team, and organization in your area. Social proof makes the next conversation ten times easier.

Script 4: The Follow-Up Email (For Any Partner Type)

Use this after a successful first conversation to confirm the partnership and set expectations.

Email Template

Subject: Free wash cards for your customers — ready to go

Hi [Partner Name],

Great talking with you [today/yesterday]. I'm excited to get this going.

Here's a quick recap of how this works:

1. I'll print and deliver your free wash cards by [date].
2. Your team hands them out to customers however feels most natural.
3. When customers scan the QR code, they get their free wash and join our text club.
4. I'll check in with you [weekly/monthly] to see how things are going and restock cards.

I've also set up a unique tracking code for your location so I can show you exactly how many customers you're sending our way. I'll share those numbers with you whenever you want.

Thanks for partnering with us. I think your customers are going to love this.

[Your Name]
[Your Car Wash]
[Phone Number]

Script 5: The Re-Engagement Script (For Partners Who Go Quiet)

Some partners start strong and fade. This script brings them back without awkwardness.

What to Say (Text, Call, or Visit)

"Hey [Name], just checking in. I wanted to see if your team needs more wash cards. We've had some great results from other partners lately — one location sent us [X] new customers last month. I'd love to make sure your team has plenty of cards so your customers don't miss out. I can drop more off [this week]. Does that work?"

Pro Tip

Never make the partner feel guilty. The re-engagement script is about restocking and sharing wins — not about pointing out that they stopped. Lead with value. Lead with results. Make them want to keep going.

Your Partnership Launch Checklist

  • Identify 10 local businesses within a 3-mile radius of your wash
  • Prioritize by customer overlap: Where do your ideal customers already go?
  • Design free wash cards with a unique QR code or keyword for each partner
  • Practice the pitch once out loud before your first visit
  • Visit one business per day for two weeks
  • Follow up with a confirmation email after every "yes"
  • Restock cards and check in with partners every 2–4 weeks
  • Track results: Which partners produce the most contacts?
  • Double down on what works. Replace what doesn't.
  • Goal: 20 active partnerships within 90 days

Remember: The operators growing fastest aren't the ones with the biggest ad budgets. They're the ones with the strongest local networks. Every partnership is a pipeline. Every pipeline compounds. Start with one partner this week. Just one. That's all it takes to set this system in motion.
